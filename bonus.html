Golden eagle

The golden eagle is the top predator in the Scottish countryside; it is a massive bird of prey that
hunts rabbits and mountain hares, but will also catch foxes and large birds like grouse.

Fun facts

  1. Golden eagle nests are on average 5-6 feet wide and 2 feet high, weighing hundreds of pounds.
  2. The largest golden eagle nest on record was 20 feet tall and 8.5 feet wide!
  3. Sometimes, golden eagles will bring herbs/aromatics into their nest. It is believed this may keep bugs away.
  4. Golden eagles may inhabit the same nest for years, or alternate between different nests.
